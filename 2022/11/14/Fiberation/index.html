Definition.

A map p:E→Bp:E\rightarrow Bp:E→B is said to have the homotopy lifting property ( HLP ) with respect to a space XXX, if:

for each pair of map f:X→Ef:X\rightarrow Ef:X→E, and a homotopy H:X×I→BH:X\times I\rightarrow BH:X×I→B starting at H0=p∘fH_0=p\circ fH0​=p∘f, there exists a homotopy H~:X×I→E\tilde{H}:X\times I\rightarrow EH~:X×I→E such that:

  • H~0=f\tilde{H}_0=fH~0​=f
  • pH~=Hp\tilde{H}=HpH~=H

The map ppp is said to be a (Hurewicz) fibration if it has HLP with respect to all spaces.
